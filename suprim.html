Performance for the 1st Quarter of 2025

Implementation Progress as of March 31, 2025

COMPONENT 3: Support to Project Implementation and Management (SUPRIM)

Sub-component 2.1: Fishers' Modern and Resilient Livelihood Investments

1. Regular Progress Meetings

Regular meetings were conducted at the BFAR Training Hall every Monday, occurring twice a month. These meetings served as a platform for discussing the progress of each component's implementation, ensuring that all stakeholders remained informed and aligned with project goals. During these sessions, updates on accomplishments, challenges encountered, and planned activities were presented, allowing for timely decision-making and coordination among team members.

FCU4A Regular Progress Meetings

2. Environmental and Social Safeguards

To facilitate the implementation of the MARLIN component and the subsequent endorsement of the necessary documentary requirements of the projects, the FCU4A conducted a write shop. The Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP) of the three priority projects for FY 2024 was conducted last February 11-12, 2025, at the BFAR Training Hall.

This plan is vital for identifying opportunities to enhance the project’s positive effects. It also outlines measures to mitigate any negative socio-economic impacts on local communities. The writeshop was a two-day event that involved the review and revision of ESMPs and their presentation.

The ESMP for the proposed livelihood projects in Cavite and Batangas for 2024 and 2025 are in various stages of preparation, with 2024 projects being prioritized. Three ESMPs were already endorsed to the RPIU. Meanwhile, other ESMPs of those livelihood projects scoped last March 2025 are currently being prepared

In general, the completion of necessary documents and endorsements remains a major bottleneck. Addressing land tenure concerns and ensuring the timely finalization of ESMPs and proposals will be critical to the smooth implementation of these initiatives

3. Information, Education, and Communication Activity

To effectively inform key stakeholders about the project objectives, the FCU4A focal persons spearheaded an Information, Education, and Communication (IEC) Campaign Activity in Cavite State University - NAIC Campus. This activity aims to enhance public awareness and understanding of the FishCoRe Project.
