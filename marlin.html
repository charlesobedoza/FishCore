COMPONENT 2: Modern and Resilient Livelihood Investments

Sub-component 2.1: Fishers' Modern and Resilient Livelihood Investments

1. Livelihood Pre-Scoping Activity

FCU4A conducted a pre-scoping activity for the proposed livelihood project on January 25–26 and February 24–27, 2025, to assess feasibility and identify required documentation. It involved interviewing fisherfolk organizations using the Livelihood Guide Questions to evaluate their capabilities, resources, skills, and interests. The Environmental and Social Screening Checklist was also used to assess potential environmental and social risks, determine possible impacts, and identify necessary mitigation measures.

Courtesy calls were made to local government officials to strengthen support and project coordination. Additionally, FCU4A conducted orientations featuring an audio-visual presentation on its goals, objectives, and implementation strategies to introduce the project to the municipality properly. The table below lists the pre-scoped livelihood projects and their potential beneficiaries/ fisherfolk organizations in Batangas and Cavite.

Livelihood Pre-Scoping Activity

The scoping activity was conducted on March 11–14, 2025, by representatives from FCU4A, RPIU, NPMO, and the World Bank in the priority coastal municipalities listed in Table 2. World Bank representatives participated as part of the 4th World Bank Implementation Support Mission, which aimed to: (1) identify and address any remaining perceived project implementation barriers that may be related to ESF and relevant requirements; (2) assess capacity and capability relevant to E&S risk management at the RPIU and FCU to support the recent changes in the review and approval process for ESMPs; and (3) provide technical advisory support from the World Bank to the RPIUs/ FCUs, as needed.

The World Bank Mission Team was composed of Jun Zeng, Senior Social Development Specialist; Junisse Palanca Mercado, Environmental Specialist; and Karen Joy Mamaril, ET Consultant.

The scoping team ensured that the Environmental and Social Screening Checklist and Livelihood Guide Questions were properly administered to potential beneficiaries. The livelihood subprojects scoped during the activity are listed below.

Sub-component 2.2: Aquaculture, Fisheries and Enterprise Development

1. FishCoRe Matching Grant Roadshow Caravan

FCU4A, in collaboration with NPMO and the RPIU, successfully conducted the FishCoRe Matching Grant Roadshow Caravan from March 24 to 28, 2025, in Nasugbu, Batangas. This initiative was part of BFAR 4A's ongoing efforts to support fisherfolk and enhance their economic resilience. The primary objectives of the activity were to: (1) provide a comprehensive orientation on the Matching Grant Program framework and implementation process; (2) offer technical guidance on the application and eligibility requirements; and (3) encourage beneficiaries to maximize the support provided under the FishCoRe Project for enterprise development and livelihood sustainability.

Representatives from targeted beneficiaries in Cavite and Batangas. The discussions focused on ensuring a clear understanding of the grant mechanism, documentation requirements, and compliance protocols.

The implementation of the Roadshow Caravan aligns with BFAR 4A’s commitment to strengthening fisheries-based livelihoods while promoting sustainable and responsible fishing practices within the region. This initiative serves as a strategic intervention to facilitate the efficient and transparent execution of the FishCoRe Matching Grant Program, ultimately contributing to the long-term economic stability of fisherfolk communities.
